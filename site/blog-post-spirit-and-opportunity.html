Spirit and Opportunity by Andrew Voss

  1. Home
  2. Spirit & Opportunity

In the summer of 2003, the Mars rovers Spirit and Opportunity were launched to Mars upon Delta II rockets from Cape Canaveral’s Launch Complex 17. The rovers were charted for 90 day exploration missions of exploring Mars’s surface and geology, and have far exceeded the call of duty. The Spirit rover functioned for over 6 years before losing contact, and the Opportunity rover still roams the surface today, making incredible discoveries and accomplishing missions directed by NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Pasadena.

Over this extended mission the NASA Spirit and Opportunity Rovers have made many ground breaking discoveries. Upon landing in the Gusev Crater, Spirit discovered basaltic rock, strongly suggesting past volcanic activity in the region. Spirit was also the first rover to discover dust devils swirling across the barren Martian surface. In their exploration, these rovers even spotted earth-like clouds in Mars’s atmosphere. Amongst the most important of the rovers’ findings is evidence of extensive water on the surface of Mars millions of years ago. In 2011 in the Endeavor Crater, the Opportunity rover found a thin and bright vein of gypsum which is speculated to have been deposited by past flowing water on Mars. These discoveries opened much discussion towards Mars’s potential to have once harbored life. A year later, Clay was found on Mars, indicating a neutral chemical pH balance- necessary for life as we know it to exist.

Since landing on Mars over 10 years ago, Spirit and Opportunity have illuminated the nature of Mars that was once so mysterious. They have paved the way for future Mars missions hoping to learn more about the Red Planet’s potentially “wet” past, and the future of the Mars environment. Although Spirit became stuck in soft soil and has since lost contact, Opportunity still roams the surface of Mars today, and holds the record for the most distance driven by any vehicle on a planet other than earth. Today Opportunity searches the surface of Mars looking for evidence of ancient life, habitability, and water. The Mars Exploration Rovers inspire their followers with every new discovery made, and remind us back on Earth of how much we still have yet to learn about the Red Planet.
