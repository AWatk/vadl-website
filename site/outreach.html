Encouraging STEM Interest

One of the main agendas of the VADL is to encourage stem interest and participation among elementary and middle school students, as well as introduce the novel work that we do. Members of the senior design team, as well as underclassmen field engineers participate in frequent community outreach events such as school and science program visits. The outreach team is even planning to present at an educational child-focused science adventure center! The team does not only cater to young students; however, they are also planning on bringing in guest speakers for the larger Vanderbilt population to learn more about the final frontier.



October 13, 2015 - Percy Priest Elementary School

A group of 6 members of the VADL visited Percy Priest Elementary School to teach their "Encore Scholars" about space exploration, perform aerospace-related science experiments, and discuss the engineering design process and how we incorporate it into our work. The event reached nearly 60 students ranging from 2nd-4th graders. The session began with the VADL getting to know the students, explaining who we are and what we do, and diving into the field of engineering. VADL members proceeded to answer questions on the fly, and finally discuss principles of aerospace design (specifically with the older students). The team covered specific topics related to forces, lift, drag, and engineering tradeoffs necessary to make a rocket fly.

The team broke from presentations to perform a fan-cart based activity with the students. The students were broken into groups of 4-6 each and given worksheets to complete relating to forces acting on a "frictionless" fan-cart. After this activity, the class reconveniened for a discussion on how the fan-cart activity relates to aerospace, and how the carts can be redesigned, a normal engineering process. Finally, the VADL took the students outside for a bottle-rocket demonstration to demonstrate how pressure (providing lift), and weight (gravity) affect the flight of even the smallest sub-scale rockets.
